Learn how to show text and images on a 128x32 I2C OLED screen.

Required material. Arduino Uno, 0.91'' 128x32 OLED Display.
OLED Layout
The 0.91'' OLED Display is a 128x32 black/white display. In order to create graphic user-interfaces, a layout proposal is proposed, which contains separated regions for the corners, sides, header, main and footer sections. Each of them have a different size and top-left corner position as indicated on the following table:
What can I do? You can draw images of predefined size on each of these regions or write some text or (single) characters.

Basic Exercises
  1. Showing text .
  2. Drawing Facilino's logo. You can import the logo at the following URL https://roboticafacil.es/facilino/blockly/img/facilino40x32.png Draw the logo in the middle of the screen. Hint: The logo's width is 40, thus, (128-40)/2=44, so the X coordinate must be 44.
  3. Drawing lines. Generate 9 lines that connect the top-left corner and a point on the right side of the screen and 32 lines that connect the top-left corner and the bottom side of the screen. The points on the right side are between the top-right corner and the bottom-right corner with a separation of 4 pixels. Similarly, the points on the bottom side of the screen are between the bottom-right corner and bottom-left corner. Hint: In a for loop, draw 9 lines from (0,0) to (128,0), (128,4), (128,8) .... (128,32). After that, in another for loop, draw 32 lines from (0,0) to (128,32), (124,32), (120,32) .... (0,32). Add a delay of 200ms between each line.
  4. Creating a layout.
