Relay

A Relay is basically a switch, that can be electrically activated or deactivated by changes in the current input. A chnage in the current of a relay´s coil (input) generates a magentic field that attracts a mechanical switch. Mechanical relays are able to handle high current switches (from 2A to 15A), but degrade over time. Multiple poles (inputs) and throws (output circuits) exist and are important to note: SPST (single-pole single-throw), SPDT (single-pole double-throw), DPST (double-pole single-throw), DPDT (double-pole) double-throw). For instance, it is very typical to use the KY-019 relay module (SPST) when you need to control a single actuator or a double relay module (two individual SPST relays)
