Stepper Motor

A Stepper Motor is a rotary actuator that is being used for a variety of applications which require high precision high torque kind of motor. It fits really well with devices where accuracy is mandatory like CNC Machines or 3D Printers, while the DC motors are being used in applications where the speed of rotation is crucial. One of the most common 5V stepper motors used in low-cost small torque applications is based on 28BYJ-48 stepper motor, which includes necessary electronics to generate motor steps using four digital pins. L298N is a H-bridge driver that can be used to control one stepper motor too.

As in the case of any other motors, a stepper motor has a rotating part which is called a rotor and a static part termed stator. The stator and rotor have magnetic poles and by energising the stator poles, the rotor moves in order to align with the stator. A rotor is a central gear-shaped piece of iron, while the stator is a set of toothed electromagnets arranged around the central gear. When the windings of the stepper motor are provided with a current with the help of an external driver circuit or a microcontroller, the rotor shaft turns in such a way that it aligns itself with the stator. The rotor is made rotating in steps which is a integer determined by the angle of movement in each step.

Coding with Facilino

Facilino provides instructions to generate steps on stepper motors. The first instruction will regulate the speed at which steps are generated (it does not actually move the motor). The second instruction will generate actual amount of indicated steps (at the previously configured speed).
