Light Dependend Resistor

A Light Dependent Resistor, shortend to LDR, or refereed to as photoresistor, is a light controlled variable resistor. Its resistance depends on the amount of visible light received. They are used to create an analog signal to control varying processes. The curve relating voltage to lumninace is non linear.

 

A Light Dependend Resistor (LDR) is made of a high resistance semiconductor. In the dark, a photoresistor can have a resistance as high as several MΩ (megaohms), while in normal light, a photoresistor can have a resistance as low as a few hundred Ω (ohms). If incident light on a photoresistor exceeds a certain frequency and intesity, photons absorbed by the semiconductor give bound electrons enough energy to jump out of their physical constrains. The resulting free electrons conduct electricity, thereby lowering resistance. The resistance range and sensitivity of a photoresistor can substantially differ among dissimilar devices. Moreover, unique photoresistors may react substantially differently to photons within certain wavelength bands. Coding with Facilino

Facilino provides several instructions to work with LDRs. The first instruction returns the raw value read from the LDR (it generates the same code as the analog read instruction in Analog Input Section. The second instruction returns the a calibrated value in percentage (a number between 0% and 100%). This will depend on the maximum and minimum lux set with the third and fourth instructions. The fifth instruction returns the calibrated value in lux.
