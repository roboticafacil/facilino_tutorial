Buttons and Switches

A switch is used in machines and processes to interact with them and they can open or close a circuit. A push-button usually consists of a plunger with a spring that need to push to open or close the switch. The difference is, that by the switch an activation switches between open and closed and stays latched. While a push-button a continous activation is required to stay in the closed state (pushbuttons with inverted function also exists).

They are one of the most basic and common electronic components you will find when you want to interact with your microcontroller. Those signals are digital and only can have the value High (ON or CLOSED) or Low (OFF or OPEN).

More complex switches that change between multiple circuit loops are also quite common in electronics, however this is out of the scope of this tutorial.

KY-004 is a popular push-button module that integrates necessary electronics to provide a digital signal to connect to your microcontroller.

Coding with Facilino

Facilino provides specific instructions to work with push-buttons. The first instruction differentiates between two of the button states: pressed and released. When the button is pressed, the first statement input is executed, while when the button is released, the second statement input is executed. The second instruction differentiates the duration of the action, so that it can distinguish a short-click and a long-click. When the user takes more time than the indicated time in milliseconds, then it will be considered as a long-click. The third instruction is a very interesting instruction because it allows the user to generate a navigation tree with menus and sub-menus (options) using two push-buttons. The first one is used as a 'next/select' button, while the second one is used as a 'back/cancel' button. The code in the General Menu will be executed as indicated by the refresh time field.
