LEDs

LED stands for Light-Emitting Diode, it is a diode that emits infrared or visible light when charged with an electric current. Visible LEDs are used in many electronic devices as indicator, in automobiles as rear-window and brake lights, and on billboards and signs as alphanumeric displays or even full-colour posters. Non visibile infrared LEDs are used for example in sensors or data transfer in for example TV-Remotes.

LEDs are susceptible to overhigh currents, so they have to be limited with a resistor. As they are diodes the polarity and the orientation in the circuit matters. By varying the composition of the semiconductors, the wavelength and therefore the colour of the emitted light can be changed. LEDs come in many different sizes and shapes.

RGB LEDs

RGB LEDs stand for Red Green Blue LEDs, they are a combination of a Red, Green and Blue Light Emitting LEDs to present the whole range of colors. They have four pins, one common cathode or anode and three pins for the individual color diode. Most of the time they have to be signaled individually but multiple layouts exist, that clever combine and access those together. KY-016 module is an example of a low-cost RGB LED that includes three resistors to avoid LEDs burn out with a common cathode.

Coding with Facilino

All Arduino boards include a built-in LED connected to pin D13, while usually in ESP boards this LED is connected to D2. Therefore, Facilino has a specific instruction to deal with this LED (first instruction below), so you can set its state to ON, OFF or even toggle. For other LEDs connected to any digital pin, you can use a digital write instruction as shown in Digital Output Section. The second instruction is used on Red/Green LEDs. These are common LEDs used, for instance, the state of a machine, where you can set it to off, red or green. The third instruction allows you to set the colour of a RGB LED by selecting 8 possible colours (black, white, red, yellow, green, cyan, blue and magenta).
