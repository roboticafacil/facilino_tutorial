DC Motor

A DC motor is a rotary electrical machines that converts direct current electrical energy into mechanical energy.The DC motor achieves this by producing changing magnetic fields to induce a continuous angular rotation that can be used to rotate for example pumps, fans, compressors or wheels. As well as conventional rotary DC motors, linear motors are also available which are capable of producing a continuous linear movement.

They require a DC voltage to regulate speed, the bigger the voltage the higher the speed. Indeed, the speed is not directly proportional to the voltage, because there are some dynamics that we should consider and the speed takes a time before reaching a steady-state value for a fixed voltage, but for most low-cost applications, this is the conventional way of dealing with DC motors because the dynamics are relatively quite fast.

Microcontrollers can not provide the required current to drive big DC motors, a transistor or specialized ICs as well as a power supply is needed. A H-bridge allows us to drive DC motors in both directions as well as to brake the motor, while half H-brdiges allows us to drive DC mototrs in just one direction. By switching the transistors (represented as switches in next figure), we allow the current to flow in one direction or another. L298N or DRV8833 are two boards with specialized IC circuits to control DC motors.

Smart Car Motor

Smart Car Motors are a very low-cost solution to drive your robot. They include a wheel and even you can attach to the other end of the motor axis the HC-020k encoder to measure the actual speed.

Coding with Facilino

Facilino provides an instruction to control the speed of DC motors (this instruction works for even L298N and DRV8833 drivers). It requires two PWM pins connected to the electronic board and the motor speed (a value between -100% and 100%).
