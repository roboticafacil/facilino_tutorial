Multisensorshield

Shields are boards that can be plugged on top of microcontroller boards extending their capabilities. The different shields follow the same principle, they are easy to mount, and cheap to produce. The multisensorshield, also known as multifunctional shield, includes is especially recommended for beginners and students that have no experience with wiring and soldering.

Multisensor shield Pinout

Multisensor shield uses Arduino Uno pinout naming and it is fully compatible with Arduino Uno board. Make sure to have the right orientation. 

Multisensor shield includes:

2 digital push-buttons (connected to pin D2 and D3).
2 LEDs, blue and red (connected to pins D12 and D13).
1 analog potentiometer (connected to pin A0).
1 digital temperature and humidity sensor, DHT11 (connected to pin D4).
1 RGB LED (connected to pins D9, D10 and D11 for red, green and blue LEDs, respectively).
1 sound passive buzzer (connected to pin D5).
1 infrared transmitter/receiver (connected to pin D6).
1 Light dependent resistor or LDR (connected to pin A1).
1 analog temperature sensor, LM35 (connected to pin A2).

It also includes expansion connectors to pins D7, D8 and A3, so you can connect additional electronics. A dedicated connector for I2C is also available, which allows you to expand even more the possibilities of using this board.

Wemos D1 R32 + Multisensor shield

Since Wemos D1 R32 uses a layout that is compatible with Arduino Uno, multisensor shield can be also used together with Wemos D1 R32 board. Please, refer to the ESP boards Section to see the compatibility pinout between Wemos D1 R32 and Arduino Uno, because naming is different. In Facilino, there's a board option that includes Wemos D1 R32 and Arduino Shield that uses the naming convention that you actually see the shield, which makes things easier.

There a very few additional considerations that you should consider to take the maximum throughput of your board. Pins D7 and D8 can be used as analog inputs too (in Arduino Uno those pins are only digital). The analog potentiometer and LDR sensors (pins A0 and A1) can NOT be used when WiFi and bluetooth is active due to a known issue using ADCs with the ESP32 processor.
